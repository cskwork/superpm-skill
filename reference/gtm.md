# GTM - go-to-market

Frameworks for getting the product to the right first market and growing from there. Win a
beachhead before boiling the ocean; every motion ties to how the customer actually buys.

## gtm-strategy

End-to-end launch plan: target segment, positioning, channels, motion, pricing/packaging,
messaging, launch milestones, and success metrics. Output: the plan + the single segment you
attack first and the metric that says the launch worked. Anchor it to a beachhead, not "everyone".

## beachhead-segment (Moore / crossing the chasm)

The narrow first market you can dominate, then expand from. Selection criteria: urgent pain,
reachable, has budget, references travel to adjacent segments, you can be the clear best option.
Output: the chosen beachhead, the expansion path out of it, and why you can win it first.

## ideal-customer-profile (ICP)

The firmographic + behavioral profile of accounts that get the most value and are easiest to
win/keep. Fields (B2B): industry, size, tech stack, trigger event, buying-committee roles, the
pain. Output: the ICP + disqualifiers (who to say no to). An ICP with no disqualifiers is too broad.
With no CRM data, source the trigger event and pain from real buyer signal (`reference/signal.md`).

## growth-loops

Self-reinforcing cycles that compound (vs a linear funnel). Form: action -> output -> reinvested
input that drives more action (e.g. content loop, viral/referral loop, paid loop where LTV >
CAC funds more acquisition). Output: 1-3 loops drawn as cycles + the bottleneck step that gates
the loop and how to widen it.

## gtm-motions

Evaluate how the product should be sold:
- **Product-led (PLG)** - self-serve, free/trial, product is the funnel. Fits low ACV, fast time-
  to-value, broad market.
- **Sales-led (SLG)** - SDR/AE motion. Fits high ACV, complex buying, security/procurement gates.
- **Hybrid / community / channel** - combinations.
Output: the recommended motion + the evidence (ACV, time-to-value, buyer complexity) and the
metrics that prove it is working (PLG: activation/expansion; SLG: pipeline/win-rate).

## competitive-battlecard

A sales-ready card per key competitor: their positioning, strengths, weaknesses, your
differentiation, landmines to set, objection-handling ("when they say X, we say Y"), and a trap
question. Output: a scannable card a rep can use live. Keep claims truthful and verifiable -
ground each in real, recent competitor and user signal (`reference/signal.md`).

## Critic completeness anchors

GTM plans name one beachhead and a launch success metric; ICPs include disqualifiers; growth
loops are drawn as reinvesting cycles with a named bottleneck; the motion recommendation is
justified by ACV/complexity evidence; battlecards stay truthful.
