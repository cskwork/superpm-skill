# GROWTH - marketing & growth

Frameworks for positioning the product and choosing the metric that focuses the team. Positioning
is a deliberate choice of context; the North Star is the one number that means delivered value.

## marketing-ideas

Generate campaign/channel ideas across the funnel (awareness -> consideration -> conversion ->
retention -> referral). Lenses: content, community, partnerships, paid, lifecycle, PLG hooks,
events. Output: 10-15 ideas tagged by funnel stage and effort, with the 3 to test first and the
metric each would move.

## positioning-ideas (April Dunford)

Position the product deliberately:
1. Competitive alternatives (what customers would use otherwise, including doing nothing).
2. Unique attributes (what only you have).
3. Value (the benefit those attributes enable).
4. Target customer who cares most about that value.
5. Market category you frame yourself in (sets the customer's reference frame and expectations).
Output: a positioning statement + the category choice and why it beats the obvious one.

## value-prop-statements

Sharp, customer-language one-liners (vs the full strategy.md canvas). Forms: "We help <segment>
<achieve outcome> by <mechanism>, unlike <alternative>." Output: 3-5 variants at different
emphases (outcome, speed, trust, cost) for testing.

## product-name

Brainstorm names against criteria: meaningful, memorable, pronounceable, available (domain/
trademark - flag as needs-check), and extensible. Output: 10-15 candidates grouped by naming
style (descriptive / evocative / invented / metaphor) + top 3 with rationale and the checks still
required. Never assert trademark/domain availability - mark it to verify.

## north-star-metric

The single metric that best captures the value customers get and predicts sustainable growth
(e.g. nights booked, messages sent, weekly active teams). Criteria: reflects customer value,
leads revenue, the team can move it. Output: the proposed North Star + 3-5 input metrics that
drive it + why it beats a vanity metric (signups, pageviews). One North Star, not a dashboard.

## Critic completeness anchors

Positioning names the competitive alternatives and a deliberate category; the North Star reflects
delivered value (not a vanity count) and has input metrics; naming and availability claims are
marked to verify, never asserted.
